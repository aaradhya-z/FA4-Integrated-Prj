Indian Satellite GSAT 11

GSAT-11 is a communication satellite launched by India in 2018 to provide broadband services across the country:
  • Weight : GSAT-11 is India's heaviest satellite, weighing 5,854 kg
  • Launch : The satellite was launched on December 5, 2018 from the Kourou launch base in French Guiana by the Ariane-5 VA-246 launch vehicle.
  • Orbit : GSAT-11 is located in a Geostationary orbit at an altitude of about 36,000 km.
  • Transponders : GSAT-11 has 40 transponders that operate in the Ku-band and Ka-band frequencies.
  • Throughput : GSAT-11 can provide a throughput of up to 16 Gbit/s.
  • Mission : GSAT-11 has a mission life of 15 years.
  • Purpose : GSAT-11 will provide high-speed data connectivity to the Indian mainland and islands, especially to rural and inaccessible areas. It will also demonstrate new generation applications.
  • Antenna Coverage GSAT-11 has multi-spot beam antenna coverage over the Indian mainland and islands.
  • Spot Beam : A spot beam is a satellite signal that is concentrated to cover a limited geographical area. The narrower the beam, the greater the power.
